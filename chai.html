Chai Masala

"Chai" is the Hindi word for tea, but in many English-speaking countries, it refers specifically to a spiced tea beverage that originated in India. Chai is a popular and traditional drink enjoyed in various forms across South Asia and beyond.

prep time

2 min.

cook time

10 min.

serving

5 servings

Tags : Breakfast Appetizer Main Drinks

instructions

step 1

Prepare the Spices: Crush the cardamom pods slightly with the back of a spoon to release their flavor. If you want a stronger spice flavor, lightly crush the cloves and break the cinnamon sticks into a few pieces. Boil Water: In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add Spices and Tea Leaves: Once the water is boiling, add the sliced ginger, crushed cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon sticks, and any optional spices. Let it simmer for 5 minutes to infuse the water with the spices. Add Tea Leaves: Add the loose tea leaves to the water and spices. Allow it to simmer for an additional 3-5 minutes. Add Milk and Sugar: Pour in the milk and add sugar. Stir well and let the chai come to a simmer. Be careful not to let it boil over. Strain and Serve: Once the chai has simmered for a few minutes and has a rich color, strain the tea into cups using a fine mesh strainer or a tea strainer to remove the tea leaves and spices. Adjust Sweetness: Taste the chai and adjust the sweetness by adding more sugar if desired. Serve Hot: Serve the chai hot and enjoy the wonderful aroma and warmth.
