Beef Meat Pie

A beef pie is a savory dish consisting of a filling made with beef and various other ingredients, enclosed in a pastry crust. The exact preparation can vary, but the general idea is to combine seasoned cooked beef with vegetables, sauces, and sometimes additional spices, encase it in a pastry shell, and then bake until the crust is golden brown.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Breakfast Appetizer Main Drinks

instructions

step 1

                Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add chopped onions and garlic, saut√© until softened.

                Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Break the meat apart with a spoon as it cooks.
                
                Stir in the diced carrots and cook for a few minutes until they begin to soften.
                
                Sprinkle the flour over the meat and vegetables, stirring well to coat evenly.
                
                Pour in the beef broth, add tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, and season with salt and pepper. Stir until the mixture thickens. Add the frozen peas and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
                
                For the Pie Crust:
                
                In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter cubes.
                
                Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
                
                Gradually add ice water, a few tablespoons at a time, and mix until the dough just comes together.
                
                Divide the dough in half. Roll out one half on a floured surface to fit the bottom of your pie dish. Place it in the dish.
                
                Pour the beef filling into the pie crust.
                
                Roll out the other half of the dough and place it over the filling. Trim the edges and crimp to seal.
                
                Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

step 2
