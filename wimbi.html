Uji Ya Wimbi

Uji" refers to a traditional East African porridge that is commonly consumed in countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, and other regions. It is a versatile dish that can be made from various grains, with millet and sorghum being popular choices.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : vegetable lunch vegetable soup food

instructions

step 1

                Rinse the Grains:

Rinse the millet or sorghum under cold water to remove any impurities.
Soak the Grains:

Soak the grains in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. This helps to soften them and reduce the cooking time.
Blend or Grind:

After soaking, drain the grains and blend them with a little water until you get a smooth paste. You can also use a grinding stone if available.
Strain the Mixture:

Strain the blended mixture using a fine sieve or cheesecloth to separate the liquid from the coarse particles. Collect the liquid in a bowl.
Cooking Uji:

In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.
Gradually add the strained millet/sorghum mixture to the boiling water, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
Simmer:

Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer, stirring occasionally. This process may take about 15-20 minutes.
Add Milk (Optional):

If using milk, add it to the porridge and continue stirring. This step adds creaminess to the uji.
Sweeten and Season:

Add sugar or honey to taste and a pinch of salt. Adjust the sweetness and saltiness according to your preference.
Serve:

Once the uji has reached your desired consistency, remove it from the heat. It should have a smooth and slightly thick consistency.
Enjoy:

Serve the uji warm in bowls. It's often enjoyed for breakfast or as a comforting snack.

ingredients

1 cup millet or sorghum (or a mix of both) 4 cups water 2 cups milk (optional) Sugar or honey to taste Pinch of salt
